Tribute to Virat Kohli

Cricketer. Leader. Inspiration.

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team, admired worldwide for his passion, talent, and dedication to the game.

Early Life

Virat was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. From a young age, he displayed an extraordinary talent for cricket, representing India at various levels, and soon made his way to the national team.

Career Achievements

From winning the Under-19 World Cup to leading India to countless victories in international cricket, Virat has left an indelible mark on the sport. He has been one of the most consistent and prolific batsmen, breaking numerous records in all formats of the game.

Unmatched Records

  • Fastest to 10,000 ODI Runs: Achieved in just 205 innings, beating Sachin Tendulkar's previous record.
  • Only Cricketer with 7,000+ Runs in IPL: Holds the record for the most runs scored in the Indian Premier League.
  • Most Centuries in Run Chases (ODI): Scored 26 centuries in successful run chases, showing his ability to perform under pressure.
  • First Player to Score Double Centuries in Four Consecutive Test Series: Known for his consistency across multiple series.
  • Highest Average for a Batsman with 50+ ODI Centuries: Holds an average of over 50 with 50+ centuries, an unparalleled feat.
